Key Nutrients for Puppies

Puppy food should be formulated to support rapid growth. Look for these essential nutrients:

  • Protein: Essential for muscle development. Should be from high-quality sources like meat.
  • Fat: Provides energy and supports brain development. Look for healthy fats like DHA.
  • Calcium and Phosphorus: Crucial for bone growth. The ratio is very important.
  • Carbohydrates: Provide energy. Should be from easily digestible sources.
  • Vitamins and Minerals: Support overall health and immune function.

Important Considerations

  • AAFCO Statement: Ensure the food is labeled with an AAFCO (Association of American Feed Control Officials) statement indicating it's suitable for growth.
  • Life Stage: Choose food specifically formulated for puppies, not "all life stages."
  • Breed Size: Consider your puppy's breed size (small, medium, large) as their nutritional needs vary. Large breed puppies need controlled calcium and phosphorus levels.
  • Ingredients: Look for high-quality, named meat sources (e.g., "chicken," not "meat meal") as the first ingredients. Avoid artificial colors, flavors, and preservatives.
  • Digestibility: Choose food with easily digestible ingredients to prevent digestive upset.
